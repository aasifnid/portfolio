01 The Problem

Growth was direct and expensive. Community reach was untapped.

Cuemath was running flagship in-person math events across the US market — competitions, workshops, discovery sessions. But awareness was limited to paid reach. The affiliate programme was the answer: community volunteers who could promote these events in their local networks and get rewarded for every registration they drove.

The events themselves were high-quality and credible — but word hadn't spread far enough. Cuemath needed people on the ground in local communities, parent groups, and schools who already trusted the brand and could vouch for it personally. A structured affiliate channel with transparent rewards was the mechanism to mobilise them.

No referral infrastructure

No mechanism for community members to refer students or promote events. Word-of-mouth was happening informally but not being tracked or rewarded.

No affiliate identity

Volunteers had no dashboard, no unique link, no visibility into how their referrals were performing. Nothing to sustain engagement after signing up.

Trust gap with new affiliates

Potential partners needed to trust both the brand and the payout system before committing. No credibility surface existed to answer "will I actually get paid?"

No admin visibility

The internal team had no way to manage affiliates, track campaign performance, or process payouts systematically. Everything was manual.

03 Key Decisions

Four decisions that shaped the programme.

Decision 01

Lead with earnings transparency, not brand values

The landing page leads with "Earn up to $2000/mo" — not Cuemath's mission. Community volunteers are motivated by both impact and income; hiding the earning potential behind brand messaging creates friction. The FAQ section explicitly names the payout structure ($2–5 per event registration, $100–200 per direct admission) so potential affiliates know exactly what they're signing up for before they commit.

Decision 02

Trust signals before the form — not after

Trustpilot reviews, the "1000+ volunteers" community stat, and the 4.8+ rating are placed before the sign-up form, not as a footer afterthought. A new affiliate asking "is this legitimate?" needs those answers before they enter personal details. The payout note ("managed at Cuemath's discretion") is also surfaced early — being upfront about limitations builds more trust than hiding them.

Decision 03

Affiliate dashboard as the retention surface

The sign-up page gets affiliates in the door. The dashboard is what keeps them active. It surfaces referral count, conversion rate, earnings, and upcoming campaigns in a single view — so affiliates have a reason to log in, check progress, and keep sharing. A dashboard that shows nothing keeps no one engaged.

Decision 04

Flexible payout timing as a trust-builder

Payouts are processed flexibly — sometimes before an event ends. This was a deliberate product decision surfaced in the FAQ: early payout signals trust to the affiliate, reduces the "will I actually get paid?" anxiety, and encourages sustained participation. It's a trust mechanism, not just an operational detail.

04 The Build

From landing to dashboard — the full journey.

Affiliate sign-up landing page

A performance page designed to convert interested community members into registered affiliates. The flow: hero (value prop + CTA) → how it works (3-step) → why partner (trust signals + earning potential) → who can join → Trustpilot reviews → FAQs → sign-up form. Each section earns the next — the visitor gets progressively more confident before being asked to commit.

Affiliate landing page — hero to sign-up form. Trust signals (Trustpilot, community size, earning transparency) placed before the form, not after.

Affiliate dashboard

Post-login surface for active affiliates. Shows their unique referral link, total referrals, conversions, pending payouts, and earnings to date. Campaign materials (flyers, videos, copy) available directly from the dashboard. Payout history visible per campaign — affiliates can see exactly what each referral earned them.

Affiliate dashboard — referral link, stats, campaign materials, and earnings all in one view.

Admin dashboard

Internal tool for the Cuemath team to manage the programme. Affiliate list with status, referral verification, campaign creation and tracking, payout processing. The admin view mirrors the affiliate view intentionally — what the affiliate sees in their dashboard corresponds to what the admin sees and can act on.

05 FAQ Design

15 questions. Every real objection answered.

The FAQ section wasn't filler — it was designed to handle every question a hesitant affiliate would have before signing up. Each question maps to a real conversion blocker.

What can I earn for?

Event promotions ($2–5 per successful registration) or direct referrals ($100–200 per admission to the tutoring programme).

When do I receive my payout?

Payouts are processed flexibly — sometimes before an event ends — to build trust and encourage continued participation.

Is there a minimum payout threshold?

Yes — $10. Once you reach this amount, you can request your payout.

What happens if someone clicks my link but signs up later?

Our tracking attributes the referral to you if they sign up within 30 days of clicking your link.

Is there any limit to how many people I can refer?

No upper limit. The more successful referrals you make, the more you earn.

07 Reflection

What I'd do differently.

No affiliate activation metric

We tracked sign-ups but not activation — how many affiliates actually shared their link after registering. A signed-up affiliate who never shares is a dead conversion. I'd instrument that gap before launch and build a nudge flow for affiliates who sign up but don't share within 48 hours.

Admin dashboard was designed last

The admin tool was scoped as the third surface and felt like it. It should have been designed in parallel with the affiliate dashboard — what the affiliate sees and what the admin manages need to be mirrors of each other. Designing them sequentially created reconciliation work at the end.

Payout trust needed more than copy

The FAQ addressed payout questions but in text only. A visual "how payouts work" timeline in the dashboard — showing assigned → verified → processed → paid — would have done more to reduce anxiety than any paragraph of copy. I'd add that in v2.
