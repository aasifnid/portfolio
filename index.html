About

Hey, thanks for stopping by

Everything I've accomplished—from discovering design to helping my friends, to designing products that matter—has been possible because I've always invested in genuine connections with people. I'm curious about how things work. Not just the surface—the systems, the friction points, why people struggle, what needs to change. I ask a lot of questions. That curiosity, combined with genuine relationships, opens doors.

Where/how it all began…

I started in mechanical engineering, certain of the path. A school friend discussed with me design as an option. Not as a career move, but as a fascinating breadth of disciplines. Photography, furniture, transportation, systems. I didn't have a master plan. I just got curious, stayed connected to people exposing me to new things, and followed where it led.

That's how I ended up at IIT Kanpur. I started by auditing design lessons and subsequently cracked an internship at the Department of Design, all while prepping for NID entrance exams—the desperation of wanting something badly, combined with genuine fascination. That's how I went from game design to product design at Cuemath—not because I decided to, but because it made sense when the opportunity arrived.

At Cuemath, I went through four organizational restructurings and layoffs in five years. Each one taught me something about resilience and what actually matters. I learned that the best work happens when you're genuinely trying to solve a real person's problem—not optimizing for metrics, but because you listened to them and understood their actual friction. That became my North Star.
