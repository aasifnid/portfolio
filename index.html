About

Created with ❤️ by Srishti Singh

I started in mechanical engineering, certain of the path. A school friend mentioned design — not as a career move, but as a fascinating breadth of disciplines. Photography, furniture, transportation, systems. I didn't have a master plan. I just got curious, stayed connected to people exposing me to new things, and followed where it led.

That's how I ended up at IIT Kanpur — auditing design lessons, cracking an internship at the Department of Design, prepping for NID. Same pattern got me from game design to product design at Cuemath. Not because I planned it, but because it made sense when the people I worked with offered it.

Five years and four restructurings later, the thing I keep coming back to is simpler than I thought: listen to the person who's stuck, understand their friction, build the thing.

What grounds me

Outside work, I read a lot about what's happening in tech and the world. I'm a foodie and love cooking — it's where I want to spend more time.

Most of the best things in my life happened through people — friends who introduced me, mentors who took a chance, colleagues who saw me make a shift. I'm aware of that. So I invest in those relationships.

How I work

I'm collaborative because you solve problems better together. I'm humble because I'm always trying to find the right answer, not defend a position. I'm resourceful—jugad is how my friends at NID describe it—because I believe in using what you have: connections, creativity, hustle, to solve real problems. I stay engaged through implementation because design isn't done at handoff. And I ask questions because I'm genuinely curious; listening deeply is how you find the real problem.

What's next

I'm looking for product design roles on consumer-facing products at scale, where design has real influence. I want to keep listening deeply, solving thoughtfully, and helping teams ship things that actually matter. Available immediately. Open to both in-office and remote setups.
