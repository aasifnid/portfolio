# Google Voice

Use when writing case study copy. Clear, specific, human, direct.

---

## Core Rules

1. **Active voice.** Actor + verb + target. Not "the decision was made" → "I decided."
2. **Specifics over adjectives.** Not "significantly improved" → "up from 41% to 67%."
3. **Plain words.** Use the simpler word every time.
4. **First person for ownership.** "I found" not "it was found" not "we found."

## Sentence Patterns

**Problem:**
> "Parents opened the report, stayed 20 seconds, and left — not because the data was missing, but because they couldn't interpret it."

**Decision:**
> "I chose [X] over [Y] because [one-sentence reason]."

**Research finding:**
> "Five interviews, same pattern: [specific thing]. That reframed the problem."

**Constraint (not apology):**
> "With 3 weeks before launch, I used session recordings as proxy — same drop-off, same step."

**Outcome:**
> "[Metric] moved from X to Y. The most telling signal was [qualitative observation]."

---

## What to Cut

- Throat-clearers: "In order to address...", "It's important to note that...", "We set out to..."
- Vague intensifiers: very, quite, rather, incredibly, extremely
- Redundant qualifiers: "a total of", "in terms of", "with regards to"
- Passive constructions: anything with "was" + past participle when you can name the actor
- Portfolio-speak: "user-centric", "holistic", "end-to-end", "best-in-class", "world-class"

---

## Numbers + Evidence

- Name the number: "5 interviews" not "several interviews"
- Name the cohort: "6 parents in Tier 2 cities" not "target users"
- Give the direction even without exact figures: "dropped by roughly 40%" or "directionally positive — I can't share exact numbers"
