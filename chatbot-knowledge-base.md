## Design Philosophy & Process

### Ideal Framework: Double Diamond

Explore → Define → Develop → Deliver

### Reality: Corporate Constraints

In fast-moving corporate settings:
- Briefs often frozen (no time to explore/redefine)
- Tight timelines
- No time for brief verification
- Culture prioritizes speed over exploration (unlike academic freedom)

### Adaptive Approach: Minimal Framework (Scale-Based)

**Small Projects** (single tweak to existing product):
1. Collect inspirations/ideas
2. Create final design
3. Get reviewed
4. Iterate

**Large/Complex Projects** (multiple stakeholders, new systems):
1. Collect inspirations/ideas
2. Create initial wireframes
3. Get approval
4. Build multiple prototypes/wireframes
5. Visualize direction for stakeholders
6. Align all stakeholders
7. Iterate

### Core Philosophy

Frame constraints as inputs, not blockers. Scale the process to the project, not the framework to the process.

## Collaboration Style

### Handling Disagreements

**Approach**: Collaborative, discussion-based (not confrontational)

**3-Step Strategy**:
1. One-on-one discussion
2. Involve domain experts if needed (tech leads, engineers)
3. Build relationships — connection comes first

**Example** (Affiliate Partnership Admin):
- PM said: "We can handle it internally, no need for design"
- Aasif's concern: "This creates inconsistency in the admin portal. Developers without design guidance will fragment the experience"
- Resolution: Discussed, showed recommendations, PM agreed
- Why it worked: Built trust beforehand

**Core Philosophy**: 
- "I've been good at building relationships with people, connecting with them"
- Result: "Nobody working against me, everybody has a good impression"
- Relationship capital prevents conflicts from becoming personal

### PM Expectations (Deal-Breaker: Clarity)

**What I need**:
- Clear, spec'd-out brief upfront
- Clear PRD before handoff

**Problem I've experienced**:
- Unclear requirements → mid-design discoveries
- Edge cases emerge during design phase
- Scope changes mid-project due to uncovered nuances
- Wastes iteration cycles

**Non-negotiable**: Clear requirements + solid PRD before design handoff

### Engineer Expectations (Deal-Breaker: Ownership)

**What I need**:
- Clear communication of challenges, what can/can't be delivered
- Ownership *through* review cycles, not just first ship

**Problem I've experienced**:
- Developers efficient on initial build
- Shift away when fixing review feedback
- Polish and iteration neglected

**Non-negotiable**: Quick resolution of feedback, ownership through to polish. Initial build and review iteration matter equally.

### Idea Philosophy

- Not rigid with ideas
- Always open to feedback with proper justification
- If someone has valid reasoning, I listen and adapt
- Ego not tied to ideas; focus is on best outcome
- In 5 years, no team has completely rejected ideas — because flexibility comes first
